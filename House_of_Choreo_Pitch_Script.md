# House of Choreo — Partnership Pitch Script
### Duration: 7–9 minutes | 10 slides | Interactive Q&A built in

---

## OPENING — Slide 1 (Cover) — ~45 sec

> "Good [morning/afternoon] everyone — thank you for the time.
>
> Quick introduction: I'm Bhuvan Singh. By training I'm an Electrical Engineer from Delhi College of Engineering, and for the last few years I've worked in market research and strategy consulting at a US-based pharma consulting firm — so a lot of my day job is spent studying how unorganised, fragmented markets behave, where the inefficiencies are, and where there's room to professionalise and scale something that currently runs on word-of-mouth and informal networks.
>
> Outside of that, I run House of Choreo — a premium wedding choreography studio. And the reason I'm pitching this to you not as a 'dance guy' but as someone who thinks about market structure is simple: **the wedding choreography space in India is a massive, completely unorganised sector** — and that's exactly the kind of problem I like solving.
>
> Today I want to walk you through who we are, the gap we've identified, and a partnership model that I think solves a real pain point for you as a planner."

**[Pause — let that land. This is your credibility anchor. Don't rush it.]**

>
> But the more important point is *why* this exists. We built House of Choreo to integrate directly into a planner's workflow — so choreography becomes one less vendor you have to chase, brief, and manage — and one more thing your clients end up raving about."

*(Gesture to the services list — don't read all 7 bullets aloud, just say:)*

## Slide 3 — THE OPPORTUNITY (Unorganised Sector Hook) — ~60 sec
**[This is your "interactive" moment — ask the room a question]**

> "Now here's the part I actually want your input on. Quick show of hands — how many of you have had a choreography vendor cancel last-minute, show up under-rehearsed, or just go completely unresponsive two days before a Sangeet?"

**[Pause for hands / reaction]**

> "Right — that's not a one-off. That's the *entire sector*. Wedding choreography in India today is almost 100% unorganised — local instructors, Instagram-discovered freelancers, college dance groups picking up gigs on the side. There's no standardisation, no accountability, no professional layer between you and the dance floor.
>
> That's the gap. We're not trying to be 'better dancers' — we're trying to be the **organised, dependable layer** that this unorganised market is missing. And for you as a planner, that directly translates to fewer fires to put out two days before the wedding."

## Slide 8 — PRICING & COMMERCIAL MODELS — ~75 sec
**[Most important slide for THEM — slow down here]**

## Delivery Notes
- **Total time check:** Slides 1–2 (~1.5 min) / Slides 3–6 (~4 min) / Slides 7–9 (~3 min) / Close (~0.5–1 min) = ~9 min, trim slide 4-6 detail if running long.
- **Interactivity:** The show-of-hands moment on Slide 3 is your strongest engagement beat — don't skip it, and genuinely pause for reactions.
- **Tone:** Lead with the consulting/market-structure framing in the intro and the unorganised-sector slide — that's what makes you sound like a strategist who *happens* to run a dance studio, not a dance instructor pitching a deck.
- **Don't read bullet points verbatim anywhere** — treat each slide's bullets as your speaker notes, not your script. Speak in full sentences, glance at slide for the next beat.
- **Have one specific wedding story ready** (a near-disaster you fixed, or a standout result) to drop in if there's a lull or a skeptical question — concrete anecdotes beat stats in this room.
